The Market

Art as an asset.

Collecting has changed — who buys, how they buy, and why. Five movements shaping global art collection, and what they mean if you're starting today.

$65Bglobal sales, 2023*

Art is a serious global market.

The Art Basel & UBS Art Market Report put worldwide art sales at roughly $65 billion in 2023 — bigger than the global recorded-music industry. Original art isn't a niche purchase; it's an established asset class with its own auctions, indices, and institutions.

~1 in 5sales now online*

Buying art online is normal now.

Online channels account for close to a fifth of global art sales in recent industry reports — a shift that stuck after 2020. Collectors routinely discover, vet, and acquire work digitally, which is why documentation, condition reports, and certified authenticity matter more than ever.

Newgeneration of buyers

Younger collectors are driving growth.

Collector surveys consistently show millennial and Gen X buyers among the most active spenders — collecting earlier, digitally, and with a taste for living artists over established estates. The first-time collector is now the market's growth engine, not its afterthought.

Indiarecord seasons

South Asian art is having its moment.

Indian auction houses have posted record-breaking seasons in recent years, with modern and contemporary South Asian works setting new highs and international houses expanding their Indian sales. Collecting Indian artists today means participating in a market still early in its arc.

Longhold, real value

Considered art has historically held value.

Industry indices tracking established artists have historically shown long-run appreciation, and art has long been used by wealth managers as a diversifier — an asset whose value doesn't move in step with equity markets. The caveat matters: appreciation follows quality, provenance, and patience, not speculation. Which is exactly why every SOA piece leaves with a certificate of authenticity and a condition report — the paperwork appreciation depends on.

*Figures as reported by industry publications, including the Art Basel & UBS Art Market Report. Art values fluctuate and past performance doesn't guarantee future results — nothing on this page is investment advice. Collect first for love of the work; treat appreciation as the long-term reward for collecting well.
